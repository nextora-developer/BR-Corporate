Company Strike-Off

Structured guidance for voluntary strike-off applications — close dormant companies properly and compliantly.

If your Sdn. Bhd. is no longer operating, a strike-off may be an appropriate way to close the company (subject to eligibility). We guide you through the process to ensure documentation is properly prepared and submissions are handled correctly.

Strike-off approval is subject to authority review and eligibility requirements.

When Is Company Strike-Off Suitable?

We help assess whether strike-off is the right route for your company.

Dormant or Inactive Companies

Typically suitable for companies that are no longer carrying on business or are fully dormant.

No Intention to Continue Operations

Suitable when there is no plan to revive or continue the company’s operations in future.

Ready to Close Properly

For directors and shareholders who wish to close the company in a structured, compliant manner.

We will advise whether strike-off is appropriate based on your company’s situation.

Why Company Strike-Off Matters

Proper closure helps reduce risk, avoid unnecessary obligations, and maintain clean statutory records.

Avoid Ongoing Compliance Burden

Helps you avoid ongoing annual obligations and administrative workload for an inactive or non-operating company.

Reduce Risk of Future Penalties

Minimises the likelihood of penalties arising from missed filings or outstanding annual obligations.

Properly Managed Company Status

Ensures the company is formally handled instead of being left dormant, outdated, or unmanaged in statutory records.

Protects Directors Through Documentation

Keeps closure steps properly documented, providing a clear paper trail for audits, compliance checks, and future reference.

Strike-Off Readiness Checklist

Key readiness points before proceeding with a strike-off application.

Company Status

  • Company is dormant / not carrying on business
  • No ongoing contracts or active operations
  • No unresolved commitments where possible

Statutory & Records

  • Key company records are organised and available
  • Any missing statutory updates are identified (if applicable)

Obligations & Liabilities

  • Outstanding liabilities and obligations are clarified
  • Bank accounts, assets and commitments addressed appropriately (where relevant)

Closure Preparedness

  • Directors / shareholders agree on the closure direction
  • Required confirmations and declarations can be provided

If you’re unsure about your company’s readiness, we can review and advise the correct steps before you proceed.
